# Ophrys

## Project charter

Ophrys is a standalone Nomadic Vistas project and the first physical installation brain within the wider Autopoiesis ecology. It is both a public artwork and an inspectable system for learning how adaptive technologies attract attention without understanding a person.

The title refers to the *Ophrys* orchid genus, whose deceptive pollination combines precise signalling with learned avoidance and variation. The project translates that operation into light, sound, movement, browser-native works, aggregate public traces and meaningful counter-action.

## Product surfaces

1. **Public field** — the front-facing exhibition, artwork archive and proposition.
2. **Studio** — a public evidence ledger showing aggregate inputs, composition cycles, provisional tactics, uncertainty, provenance and counter-readings.
3. **Operator** — a protected control surface for models, cycles, retention, curation and publication decisions.
4. **Composition organ** — a server-side GPT‑5.6 pipeline that produces strict artwork scores for human review.

## Audiences

- cultural visitors seeking a compelling contemporary-art encounter;
- students, educators and public-learning programmes examining AI persuasion and agency;
- artists, curators and researchers studying generative and adaptive systems;
- operators responsible for safety, curation, rights and maintenance.

## Success criteria

- A visitor can experience the proposition before reading an explanation.
- One complete signal → aggregate observation → provisional interpretation → adaptation → refusal loop is demonstrable.
- Studio makes the system inspectable without exposing private reasoning or visitor dossiers.
- Operator is deny-by-default and secrets never enter public assets.
- GPT‑5.6 creates bounded, provenance-ready artwork candidates rather than generic prose.
- Human approval is required before public or exhibition status.
- The Build Week contribution is runnable, documented, tested and clearly distinguished from prior concept work.

## Governing sentence

> Ophrys does not secretly discover who a visitor is; it exposes how little evidence a machine needs before it begins acting as if it knows.
