# Education encounter protocol

## Learning outcome

After one Lure → Reveal → Counter-read sequence, a visitor can distinguish a coarse aggregate observation from a provisional system interpretation and verify that refusal changes the shared repertoire. The encounter does not ask the visitor to disclose personal information or treat their response as evidence of an inner state.

## Five-minute public sequence

1. **Lure — notice before explanation (one minute).** Ask the visitor to notice the live field and name what is materially present: motion, density, tempo, tilt and the active lure. Do not ask why it attracted them or interpret their behaviour.
2. **Reveal — inspect the layers (two minutes).** Read the five labelled layers together. Compare the coarse approach, attention and refusal totals with Ophrys's provisional interpretation, explicit uncertainty, bounded artistic mapping and retained human curatorial responsibility.
3. **Counter-read — test agency (one minute).** Invite the visitor to activate **Refuse this lure**, or to decline and watch another participant. A completed counter-read must name the suppressed lure, show its replacement and increase the collective revision.
4. **Debrief — contest the reading (one minute).** Ask: “What did the system observe?”, “What did it add as interpretation?”, and “What changed when the reading was refused?” A valid answer does not need to agree with Ophrys; disagreement is part of the work.

## Observable completion evidence

- The active lure is named before the explanatory layers.
- Observation, interpretation, uncertainty, artistic choice and human responsibility remain separately labelled.
- Refusal changes the bounded repertoire rather than merely displaying confirmation copy.
- The Studio records only a coarse aggregate refusal count and shows the field in `counter-read` phase.
- The visitor can state that successful attraction is not evidence of identity, motive or emotion.

## Facilitation and access boundary

Participation is optional. A visitor may observe the sequence without activating the control, use the keyboard, or enable reduced motion. Facilitators must not record individual answers, assign psychological meaning to a response, imply that the system recognised a person, or present a generated candidate as curator-approved. If the live state is unavailable, the interface says so and the facilitator must not claim that a counter-action occurred.

For a deterministic technical verification of the same state transition, run `npm run judge:smoke` and follow `docs/JUDGING.md`.
