# Public-data and participation boundary

Ophrys studies attraction without converting public behaviour into claims about identity or interior truth.

## Required boundary

- Aggregate tactics, not individual profiles.
- No facial retention or recognition.
- No demographic or disability inference.
- No emotion classification.
- No compelled disclosure.
- No silent export of public traces to third-party model services.
- Observation, voluntary self-report and artistic interpretation remain distinct.

## Meaningful refusal

Leaving, withholding, correcting, deleting or disrupting a reading are forms of public intelligence. They should affect the installation rather than being categorised as failed engagement.

A prototype must therefore include at least one inspectable refusal mechanism, such as:

- a physical switch that removes recent events;
- a visible reset or forgetting action;
- a counter-signal that suppresses a learned tactic;
- an annotation that disputes the system's interpretation;
- a collective action that introduces ambiguity into the observation layer.

## Evidence discipline

Keep three records separate:

1. **Observed event:** a coarse, time-bounded occurrence.
2. **Voluntary account:** something a participant explicitly chooses to report.
3. **Artistic interpretation:** a provisional reading made by the system or artist.

The system may adapt from the first and be challenged by the second, but it must never present the third as objective knowledge.

## Prototype review questions

- Can a visitor recognise that sensing is taking place?
- Can they understand its approximate resolution and limits?
- Can they decline or alter participation without penalty?
- Does refusal produce a real system change?
- Are retained events necessary, minimal and automatically expired?
- Can the work function when sensing is disabled?
