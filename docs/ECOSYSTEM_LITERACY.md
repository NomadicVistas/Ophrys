# Ecosystem literacy protocol

## Learning outcome

After a five-minute Studio reading, a visitor can distinguish a **node**, a declared **relation**, a provisional **interpretation**, a **simulated output**, and a recorded **human decision** without disclosing personal information.

The browser displays current examples from the bounded public projection. It does not ask for answers, retain quiz results, or infer whether a visitor understood the protocol. Its automated rubric verifies the presence of inspectable technical evidence only.

## Five-minute sequence

1. **Node — name the record.** Find the runtime-field node and name its type and current recorded state. A node is a bounded record, not a person or profile.
2. **Relation — follow the declared edge.** Follow one edge and read its label and evidence. The edge records a specific declared connection; it does not prove causation, similarity, authorship, approval, or understanding.
3. **Interpretation — separate what was counted from what was added.** In a candidate lifecycle, compare coarse aggregate totals with the provisional lure hypothesis. If no candidate lifecycle exists, the Studio must say so rather than invent an interpretation.
4. **Simulated output — test the physical claim.** Inspect the mapping version, source revision, input digest, transport and hardware-action fields. A simulator frame says what bounded light and sound values would be requested; it does not show that a lamp, speaker or installation rendered them.
5. **Human decision — locate authority.** Find a curatorial decision node, its rationale-bearing edge and the artwork status transition. Only the protected human gate may change publication state; the role label is not proof of reviewer identity or deliberative quality.

## Observable technical evidence

- The five concepts have separate labels, prompts, current examples and limits.
- Every current relation example has both endpoints in the bounded topology projection.
- A generated interpretation appears only when an actual candidate lifecycle exists.
- The simulated-output example requires `hardwareAction: false`, `transport: none` and a projected physical-output node.
- The decision example requires a graph-closed decision node and governance edge.
- The Studio reports how many checks have current evidence and labels this as a technical rubric, not a learner score.

The deterministic `npm run judge:smoke` fixture supplies all five evidence types and requires a `5 / 5` technical result without a paid model request, persistent database, hardware action or visitor response.

## Facilitation and participation boundary

Participation is optional. A visitor may read, discuss, observe another person, or decline the sequence. A facilitator may ask visitors to point to the evidence and contest its labels, but must not record answers, rank comprehension, infer capacity or motive, or describe the technical rubric as research evidence about learning.

This protocol is public AI-literacy material, not human-subject research, a psychological assessment or proof of educational effectiveness. Any later evaluated programme requires a separate human-approved research and data-governance plan.
