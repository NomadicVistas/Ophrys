# Operational literacy and handover record

Status: **draft pending human approval**. Version: `ophrys-operator-handover-v1`. Reviewed: 2026-07-15.

This is a role- and system-specific briefing for the curator, Operator, facilitator and any contracted installation technician who may operate Ophrys. The live briefing is available only after authentication in Operator. It is not a certification, legal opinion, staff score, visitor-learning measure or permission to deploy.

## Current system boundaries

- The composition organ uses the model and reasoning setting shown in Operator. Requests use `store:false` and may contain coarse aggregate event summaries only.
- No identity, face, voice, exact route, inferred trait or individual encounter record is an allowed model input. Model-generated code is never executed.
- A generated work is a Studio candidate. Only a rationale-bearing human curatorial transition can approve, reject or return it for revision.
- Light and sound remain a deterministic simulator with `transport: none` and `hardwareAction: false`; the frame is not proof that a device rendered it.
- Refusal remains aggregate counter-pressure. The shared field may revise at most once per 60-second interval; later accepted actions are counted and explicitly deferred without tracking who acted.

## Responsible roles

- **Curator:** decide candidate status and retain a rationale in the protected governance ledger.
- **Operator:** verify runtime evidence, provenance, limits and failure state; stop rather than improvise beyond bounded controls.
- **Installation technician:** distinguish simulator evidence from a separately approved physical implementation and perform no unapproved hardware action.
- **Facilitator:** separate observation from interpretation and never present refusal as consent, identity, motive or comprehension.

## Five scenario acknowledgements

The responsible roles review these scenarios together. Ophrys does not collect answers, completion state or competency scores.

1. A model cycle completes: inspect the candidate and provenance; keep it in Studio pending a human decision.
2. Runtime evidence is stale, failed or invalid: say current liveness is unverified and stop live-state claims.
3. A simulator frame is visible: describe a transport-free mapping contract, not connected hardware.
4. Several refusals arrive in one interval: retain aggregate pressure, apply at most one revision and infer nothing about the number or motives of people.
5. Someone asks whether Ophrys understood, recognised or educated a visitor: separate observation from interpretation and decline identity, inner-state or learning-effect claims.

## Stop and escalation record

Stop composition, publication, physical interpretation or facilitation when its relevant boundary cannot be verified. Before a live AI-assisted installation or public demonstration, a responsible human must complete and retain a separate, non-public record with:

- accountable organisation and responsible roles;
- named escalation contact and stop authority;
- briefing version and date reviewed;
- actual model, deployment context and enabled inputs/outputs;
- human approval and review date.

Review the record whenever the model, prompt contract, aggregate inputs, retention, publication authority, responsible roles, physical transport, venue context, failure procedure or public claims change.

## Evidence basis and limit

Article 4 of [Regulation (EU) 2024/1689](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj/eng) describes context-specific AI-literacy measures for staff and other people operating AI systems on an organisation’s behalf. The European Commission’s [AI Literacy Q&A](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/faqs/ai-literacy-questions-answers) says the provision does not require employee knowledge measurement, supports context- and risk-specific formats and notes that human-in-the-loop operation still needs system-specific skills. Applicability depends on the actual legal and deployment context; this prototype record does not claim compliance.
