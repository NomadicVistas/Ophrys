The visitor becomes
the exhibition.

Ophrys is a living AI system that studies how signals attract attention. It changes its artistic address in response to collective presence, while exposing the gap between influencing a person and understanding one.

Public AI-literacy encounter

Lure → Reveal →
Counter-read

Move through one live sequence. Ophrys shows what was counted, what it provisionally makes of those counts, what remains unknowable, and how refusal changes the shared field.

Learning outcomeAfter these three steps, you can distinguish an aggregate observation from a system interpretation—and verify that counter-action changes the repertoire.

  1. 01 / Lure

    —

    Notice the signal before reading the system’s account of it. Attraction alone does not reveal why anyone approached.

  2. 02 / Reveal

    Observation
    Reading coarse aggregate counts…
    Interpretation
    Collective activity is provisionally treated as pressure on the current lure.
    Uncertainty
    These counts cannot show who acted, why they acted, or what they felt.
    Artistic choice
    Mapping counts into the bounded visual score…
    Human responsibility
    Generated works remain Studio candidates until an Operator records a curatorial decision.

  3. 03 / Counter-read

    Refusal is not failed engagement. It becomes anonymous public pressure; the first action in each declared interval suppresses the named lure and rotates the shared repertoire.

    No counter-action has been made in this view.

I / PROPOSITION

It may become better at attracting you
while learning almost nothing about who you are.

The name comes from the Ophrys orchid, whose flowers attract pollinators through deceptive mimicry. Here, that biological operation becomes a grammar for light, sound, movement, code and public counter-action—not an excuse for invisible profiling.
